áƒ¦Background and Significance of the Studyáƒ¦

Composting is a process of recycling organic matter such as fruit peels, vegetable peels,

eggshells, or any other food scraps and turning them into a fertilizer that will help enrich the

soil to help our plant grow. Composting helps in gardening because it reduces the need for chemical

fertilizers which can lead to serious issues that will affect the growth of our plants. Composting

allows our plants to develop healthier roots into the soil, makes any soil easier to work with, helps

alter the soils structure making it unlikely to erode or spatter, compost can also hold nutrients tight

enough for it not to wash out but loosely enough to allow the plants to take up the nutrients that it needs,

and many other benefits. We can use vegetable or fruit peels as our compost, in this experiment, we made use

of onion peels as it contains a good amount of nutrients such as, nitrogen, potassium, calcium, iron, magnesium,

phosphorus, and more which are all essential to the healthy growth of plants. Through the benefits that composting

brings in plant growth, our Alugbati plant can make use of these making it grow faster and healthier as it was able

to get all the vitamins and nutrients that it needs to become a healthy plant. Composting is beneficial to our

environment as well because it helps reduce the greenhouse emissions at landfills, creates a sustainable agricultural

system, promotes the uptake of carbon dioxide through vegetation, makes our environment more resilient to the effects

of climate change, and more. Our partner institution, the Badjao community, can make use of the benefits from composting

through planting. They can plant fruits or vegetables like the Alugbati plant for example since it is a great source of

nutrients and through composting they will be able to grow a healthy plant. Since the Badjao community are struggling

to find food, planting would be of great help to them since it is a fast and easy way of getting access to healthy food

with the help of composting. Not only will they be able to eat well but they will also gain lots of nutrients and

vitamins such as, magnesium, zinc, iron, calcium, phosphorus, fiber, etc. which are present in vegetables and fruits,

they can also get some exercise through this planting activity which will help them attain a healthier and stronger

immune system. Lastly, the Badjao community will also be able to contribute in the process of attaining a healthier and

better environment, given that composting has a lot of benefits to the environment.

áƒ¦REVIEW OF RELATED LITERATUREáƒ¦

In general, compost is decayed organic matter used as fertilizer. One of the things that compost fertilizer does to plants to help them grow is that compost loosens the soil so that the roots can absorb nutrients. Compost also increases the soil's capacity to hold nutrients while supplying the soil with essential nutrients. In our project, we chose banana peels as our compost material as it is very easy to compost and requires less attention than when composting food scraps. Fruit waste, such as bananas and their peels, average 35 parts carbon to 1 part nitrogen, so they are quite close to the recommended ratio on their own. You only need to add a few grass clippings, which average 20 to 1, and you will have created a nice balance. Banana peels in compost adds healthy organic material, which helps the compost retain water and make the soil lighter when added to your garden. In addition to this, banana peels will break down quickly in compost, which allows them to add these important nutrients to the compost much more quickly than some other compost materials.
